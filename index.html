Jose M. Faleiro

I am a first year computer science Ph.D. student in the Dedis group at Yale University. My advisor is Bryan Ford. My research interests are operating systems, distributed systems and security. I am also interested in building tools for debugging.

I am currently working on mitigating the harmful effects of side-channels using a combination of deterministic execution and I/O pacing.

Prior to Yale, I worked as a Research Developer at Microsoft Research India. I primarily worked with Rigorous Software Engineering and the Advanced Development Group. I spent a significant portion of my time working on CScale and DebugAdvisor. My work on DebugAdvisor has been deployed within Microsoft's Lync team, for which I received a Microsoft Research Tech Transfer Award.

I have an undergraduate degree in Computer Science from Birla Institute of Technology and Science (BITS), Pilani.
