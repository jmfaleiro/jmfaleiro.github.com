Research

Current

I am currently working on the practical uses of determinism in security and correctness of systems. Deterministic programming models such as Kahn Process Networks, guarantee that the output of a program is dependent on only its explicit inputs. We are exploring the use of determinism in controlling information leakage through timing side-channels.

Past

Prior to joining Yale, I spent a year at Microsoft Research India.

Projects I spent a significant amount of time on:

CScale: Distributed systems are notoriously hard to build and debug. An application's core logic is often embedded in code which manages partitioning, fault-tolerance and other nasty parts of a system. CScale is a stateful, declarative distributed programming model that lets programmers focus on their applications, while guaranteeing consistency and fault-tolerance.

DebugAdvisor: The repositories of large-scale software projects contains information such as bugfixes, source commits, and the people associated with them. DebugAdvisor is a recommendation system which correlates information about a current bug with the history of the project in a repository. It recommends previous fixes and commits which may be related to the bug, and even people the programmer may consult to get help!
